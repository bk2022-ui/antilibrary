What it is

An engine that takes any book inventory and surfaces its intellectual structure — threads, clusters, patterns, and a reveal of what the collection says about how the person thinks. Then it puts that structure in front of the world.

Five steps. Five agents. Each named in English and Hindi. The Hindi names are not translations — they carry meaning rooted in Sanskrit that deepens what the English word points at.

The five steps
01
Collect
Sangrah
संग्रह
Parse raw input → structured, enriched inventory
Takes any form of input — shelf photographs, CSV export, Goodreads list, conversation — and produces a structured inventory entry per book. Enriches with cover images, ISBNs, and descriptions from Google Books and Open Library. Flags low-confidence entries for human review before classification runs. Canonicalizes multiple editions as distinct siblings, not duplicates — the arrangement of editions is itself data.

Is not: classification, decomposition, or curation. Every parseable book enters. No categories, no register, no atomic units assigned here.
Trigger: new file uploaded Output: sangrah-staging.json → inventory.json
02
Classify
Parichay
परिचय
Classify the book, then decompose it into atomic units
Two passes, in order.

Pass 1 — Classify. Gives every book its full coordinates: register (empirical → contemplative), density, form, lineage, author tradition and origin, primary and secondary domains, topic and subtopics. A book belongs to multiple categories simultaneously — Business AND Sports, not one or the other. Classification is calibrated against 6 similar books already in the inventory, so vocabulary stays consistent with established patterns.

Pass 2 — Decompose. Breaks the book into 3–7 typed atomic units: concept, framework, story, claim, or lens. Each unit is transferable — it can be lifted from the source book and placed next to ideas from completely different books. Units are stated at face value (exact mechanism, 2–3 sentences). No abstractions, no connections to other books, no tensions. That is Manthan's job.

Before either pass, Parichay enriches with three sources: description (from Sangrah), table of contents from Google Books, and a Wikipedia summary. Chapter titles are the book's own decomposition — richer than any blurb.

Is not: connection-forming, abstraction, or curation. All books that pass Sangrah get classified. Filtering is Darshan's job.
Trigger: per new book in staging Output: parichay-staging.json → inventory.json + ideas.json
03
Churn
Manthan
मंथन
Surface threads, clusters, patterns — the Adirondacks search
Named after Samudra Manthan — the Hindu myth of churning the cosmic ocean until treasures rise to the surface. Runs seven lenses across the classified inventory: depth strata, tension pairs, founding figures, register distribution, era concentration, hidden collections, tradition mapping. Then runs the random probe — an Adirondacks search that selects edge books as seeds and finds groupings that systematic lenses miss. Human priming guides and corrects throughout. The churn is not analysis — it is encounter.
Trigger: N new books classified (batch) Output: analysis.json
04
Curate
Darpan
दर्पण
The mirror — you see yourself; then you pull what to show
Darpan means mirror — you only see yourself in it. Internal-facing: the curator's complete self-knowledge, in two layers.

Lekha-Jokha (लेखा-जोखा) — the numbers: book counts, atomic units, distributions, enrichment coverage, pipeline state, trends. Non-negotiable, objective, always current — a derived view of the canonical JSON.

Darshan (दर्शन) — the meaning: Manthan's findings understood as a portrait, where priming happens.

Curate = Darpan: knowing everything that is there, the curator pulls a subset forward to Pradarshan. Knowing precedes pulling.
Lekha-Jokha: auto-regenerates per run Darshan + pull: human Output: darpan-stats.json + selection config
05
Visualize
Pradarshan
प्रदर्शन
You show it to others — the public act
Pradarshan means to show to others: pra (forth) + darshan (seeing). Darpan was the private mirror; Pradarshan is the public window. The web layer — pages, views, interactions — that a visitor experiences. Reads from inventory.json, manthan-analysis.json, and the selection config the curator pulled from Darpan. Renders what was pulled, in the order chosen. Pradarshan is a client: it reads, never writes. Any rendering layer that reads the same data files is a valid Pradarshan implementation.
Trigger: deployment (always live) Output: the website

Output files

The living record. One entry per book. Produced by Sangrah, enriched by Parichay. Never restructured — only extended. Currently 703 books.
ideas.json
The atomic layer. 3–7 Lego blocks per book — transferable ideas that seed Manthan's deep probe. Produced by Parichay.

The structured analysis. Threads (Sutra), clusters (Samuha), patterns, tension pairs, founding figures, the reveal. Produced by Manthan, shaped by priming.
Outside the five steps

Gaps / Khoj — खोज

The forward-looking loop. Sits outside the five steps and feeds back into Sangrah as acquisition targets.

Reactive gaps — surfaced by Manthan: a thread missing its anchor book, a cluster pulling toward a book not yet owned, a tradition entirely absent from the collection.

Proactive gaps — require human intention: where is this library going? Two people with identical libraries have different gaps because they are headed somewhere different. Khoj cannot be automated.

Naming convention
Every step carries its name in English and Hindi. The Hindi names are not translations — they carry meaning from Sanskrit that the English word only approximates. English for clarity, Hindi for identity. If a third language becomes relevant for a specific library, it slots in as a third column.

Darpan / Darshan / Pradarshan — the mirror, the seeing, the showing: Darpan (दर्पण) is the mirror — you only see yourself in it (Step 4, internal). Within it, Lekha-Jokha (लेखा-जोखा) is the stock-taking — the numbers — and Darshan (दर्शन) is seeing for yourself — the meaning. Pradarshan (प्रदर्शन) means to show to others — pra (forth) + darshan (Step 5, external). The curator knows everything in the mirror, then pulls a subset to the window. Count yourself → understand yourself → show yourself.

Manthan: Named after the Hindu myth of Samudra Manthan — churning the cosmic ocean until treasures rise to the surface. The Churn step is an Adirondacks search: many lenses, random probes, human priming. Many peaks of unknown height, not a single summit.
Design principles
No acronymsNames are direct English + direct Hindi.
Schema over codeThe analytical output is data, not logic. Any visualization layer can read it.
Confidence is explicitLow-confidence entries surface for human review. Never silently promoted.
Editions are distinctMultiple copies of the same text are siblings, not duplicates. The arrangement is data.
Priming is first-classThe engine without human priming produces shallow results. The priming interface is not optional.
Gaps are humanKhoj cannot be automated. Do not attempt to generate gaps from inventory analysis alone.
Books are multi-nodeA book belongs to multiple threads, clusters, and patterns simultaneously. No forced exclusivity.
The random probe is essentialSystematic search finds obvious peaks. The Adirondacks terrain requires random jumps.
Curate = DarpanCuration is not a separate step. The curator knows everything in the mirror, then pulls what to show. Knowing precedes pulling.
Mirror before windowDarpan is internal (you see yourself); Pradarshan is external (the world sees a chosen subset).
Lekha-Jokha is non-negotiableThe numbers are always present, always current, never interpreted — a derived view, never a new source of truth.
Pradarshan is a clientIt reads, never writes. Any rendering layer reading the same data files is valid.
