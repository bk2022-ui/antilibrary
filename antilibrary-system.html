What it is

An engine that takes any book inventory and surfaces its intellectual structure — threads, clusters, patterns, and a reveal of what the collection says about how the person thinks. Then it puts that structure in front of the world.

Five steps. Five agents. Each named in English and Hindi. The Hindi names are not translations — they carry meaning rooted in Sanskrit that deepens what the English word points at.

The five steps
01
Collect
Sangrah
संग्रह
Parse raw input → structured, enriched inventory
Takes any form of input — shelf photographs, CSV export, Goodreads list, conversation — and produces a structured inventory entry per book. Enriches with cover images, ISBNs, and descriptions from Google Books and Open Library. Flags low-confidence entries for human review before classification runs. Canonicalizes multiple editions of the same text as distinct siblings, not duplicates.
Trigger: new file uploaded Output: inventory.json
02
Classify
Parichay
परिचय
Dimension every book; decompose into atomic ideas
Gives every book its coordinates across multiple axes: register (empirical → contemplative), density, form, lineage, era, author tradition and origin, primary and secondary domains, precise topic and subtopics. Then decomposes each book into 3–7 atomic Lego blocks — transferable ideas that can be lifted from the source book and placed next to ideas from completely different books. These blocks seed Manthan's deep probe.
Trigger: per new book Output: inventory.json (classified) + ideas.json
03
Churn
Manthan
मंथन
Surface threads, clusters, patterns — the Adirondacks search
Named after Samudra Manthan — the Hindu myth of churning the cosmic ocean until treasures rise to the surface. Runs seven lenses across the classified inventory: depth strata, tension pairs, founding figures, register distribution, era concentration, hidden collections, tradition mapping. Then runs the random probe — an Adirondacks search that selects edge books as seeds and finds groupings that systematic lenses miss. Human priming guides and corrects throughout. The churn is not analysis — it is encounter.
Trigger: N new books classified (batch) Output: analysis.json
04
Curate
Darshan
दर्शन
You see it — the private editorial act
Darshan means to see — for yourself. Before anything is shown to the world, the curator first beholds everything Manthan surfaced. Out of that private seeing comes the editorial decision: what is ready, what is private, what is the right order, how much to reveal at once. Not everything Manthan found will be shown. A museum director does not hang every work in the collection. Darshan is that decision — and it can never run autonomously. The seeing is always the curator's.
Trigger: human decision Output: curation config for Pradarshan
05
Visualize
Pradarshan
प्रदर्शन
You show it to others — the public act
Pradarshan means to show to others: pra (forth) + darshan (seeing). Darshan was the private beholding; Pradarshan is the public showing. The web layer — pages, views, interactions — that a visitor experiences. Reads from inventory.json, analysis.json, and the Darshan curation config. Renders what Darshan selected, in the order Darshan decided. Pradarshan is a client: it reads, never writes. Any rendering layer that reads the same data files is a valid Pradarshan implementation.
Trigger: deployment (always live) Output: the website

Output files
inventory.json
The living record. One entry per book. Produced by Sangrah, enriched by Parichay. Never restructured — only extended. Currently 703 books.
ideas.json
The atomic layer. 3–7 Lego blocks per book — transferable ideas that seed Manthan's deep probe. Produced by Parichay.
analysis.json
The structured analysis. Threads (Sutra), clusters (Samuha), patterns, tension pairs, founding figures, the reveal. Produced by Manthan, shaped by priming.
Outside the five steps

Gaps / Khoj — खोज

The forward-looking loop. Sits outside the five steps and feeds back into Sangrah as acquisition targets.

Reactive gaps — surfaced by Manthan: a thread missing its anchor book, a cluster pulling toward a book not yet owned, a tradition entirely absent from the collection.

Proactive gaps — require human intention: where is this library going? Two people with identical libraries have different gaps because they are headed somewhere different. Khoj cannot be automated.

Naming convention
Every step carries its name in English and Hindi. The Hindi names are not translations — they carry meaning from Sanskrit that the English word only approximates. English for clarity, Hindi for identity. If a third language becomes relevant for a specific library, it slots in as a third column.

Darshan / Pradarshan: Darshan (दर्शन) means to see for yourself. Pradarshan (प्रदर्शन) means to show to others — pra (forth) + darshan. You cannot show to others without first seeing for yourself. Step 4 is the private act; Step 5 is the public one.

Manthan: Named after the Hindu myth of Samudra Manthan — churning the cosmic ocean until treasures rise to the surface. The Churn step is an Adirondacks search: many lenses, random probes, human priming. Many peaks of unknown height, not a single summit.
Design principles
No acronymsNames are direct English + direct Hindi.
Schema over codeThe analytical output is data, not logic. Any visualization layer can read it.
Confidence is explicitLow-confidence entries surface for human review. Never silently promoted.
Editions are distinctMultiple copies of the same text are siblings, not duplicates. The arrangement is data.
Priming is first-classThe engine without human priming produces shallow results. The priming interface is not optional.
Gaps are humanKhoj cannot be automated. Do not attempt to generate gaps from inventory analysis alone.
Books are multi-nodeA book belongs to multiple threads, clusters, and patterns simultaneously. No forced exclusivity.
The random probe is essentialSystematic search finds obvious peaks. The Adirondacks terrain requires random jumps.
Darshan is privateThe curator sees for themselves first. Only then does Pradarshan show the world.
Pradarshan is a clientIt reads, never writes. Any rendering layer reading the same data files is valid.
